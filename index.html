Why It Matters

Your health costs will rise.
Will your savings?

Nigeria is experiencing a quiet but serious chronic disease crisis. Hypertension, diabetes, Chronic Kidney Disease, and cardiovascular disease are no longer diseases of old age — they are increasingly affecting working-age Nigerians, many of whom are undiagnosed and unprotected. The cost of managing these conditions is real, ongoing, and significant. A health savings account is not optional. It is essential.

8M
8 Million Nigerians With Diabetes

Nearly 3 in 4 are undiagnosed. Managing diabetes costs ₦80,000–₦200,000 per year in medication, monitoring, and specialist visits — and rising. Every year, for life.

1 in 4
Adults Have Hypertension

It presents silently. Medication is lifelong. Untreated, it leads to stroke, heart failure, and kidney damage — the most expensive outcomes in Nigerian healthcare.

Rising
Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

CKD affects 1 in 7 adults globally. In Nigeria, poor early detection means most cases are caught late. Dialysis alone costs ₦1,500,000–₦3,000,000 annually.

54 Yrs
Life Expectancy

54 Yrs — Nigeria's Average Life Expectancy. Most Nigerians will face their highest health costs in the final decade of their life with no savings, no insurance, and no plan. The time to build that fund is now — not later.

₦480k
Avg. Catastrophic Health Event

A single hospitalisation, surgery, or cancer diagnosis can cost a family ₦300,000–₦2,000,000 out of pocket. Most Nigerian families are one serious illness away from a financial crisis.

Earlier
Chronic Disease Striking Younger

Hypertension and type 2 diabetes are increasingly diagnosed in Nigerians in their 30s and 40s. Lifestyle changes, stress, and poor routine care are accelerating this trend. Prevention and early access matter enormously.

A health savings account is not a luxury. It is a necessity.

The earlier you start, the more your savings grow. Even ₦5,000 a month, consistently saved and earning returns, builds a meaningful buffer against the inevitable costs of aging and illness.

Common Questions Answered
Myth #1

"Why do I need this?"

Because health costs only rise with age. Medication for diabetes, hypertension, or CKD can cost ₦80,000–₦500,000 annually. A growing health savings account means you are while you have full healthcare access. It's a better financial model — not magic.

Myth #2

"What if returns drop?"

Your healthcare access never depends on returns. Your deposits are always yours. Returns are a bonus on top of real healthcare access. Your health savings account is not a product — it is a life decision.

Myth #3

"Telemedicine isn't real healthcare"

Real doctors. Real diagnoses. Real prescriptions. For 70% of health issues — infections, chronic follow-ups, consultations — telemedicine is faster, cheaper, and just as effective.
